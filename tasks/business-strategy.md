# Business Strategy — Logistic App

## Project Context

- **Client (Phase 1)**: Friend studying Logistics Engineering at university
- **Client (Phase 2)**: Factory (end user)
- **Goal**: Build a logistics/container management desktop app in two phases

---

## Phase Plan

| Phase | Client | Price | Goal |
|-------|--------|-------|------|
| 1 | University student (friend) | ~5,000 THB | Pass her university project |
| 2 | Factory | 30-day trial → paid license | Sell extended features |

---

## Phase 1 — University Submission

**Budget**: 5,000–8,000 THB (fixed scope)

**Key rule**: Define "done" clearly before starting.
- Which screens are included?
- Which reports/outputs?
- What data does she need to demonstrate?

**Risk**: Scope creep. Without a clear definition, 5,000 THB becomes unlimited free work.

---

## Phase 2 — Factory Deployment

**Trial**: 30 days free (not 7 days — factories run on monthly cycles, too short to evaluate)

**Pricing options**:
- Monthly license: 3,000–5,000 THB/month per factory
- One-time license: 30,000–50,000 THB + support contract

**Features to add for factories** (paid):
- Container settings configuration
- Product settings configuration
- More advanced config/customization

---

## Ownership & IP — Important

Clarify before Phase 2 begins:
- You (developer) retain IP rights
- If the factory wants the software, you get paid **separately** — not via your friend
- Your friend cannot resell or hand off the software without your agreement

---

## Biggest Risk

Building two products for two different customers at once. Keep Phase 1 minimal and clean. Do not over-engineer for factory use — that is Phase 2's job.
